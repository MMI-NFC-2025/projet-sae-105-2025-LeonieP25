Nettie Stevens' Theory

In 1905, Nettie Stevens discovered that biological sex is determined by specific chromosomes (X and Y), revolutionizing our understanding of human heredity.

Explanation and popularization of her discovery (XY sex chromosomes)

By studying insects, Nettie Stevens discovered that biological sex is determined by a difference in chromosomes. She observed that females have two X chromosomes, while males have one X and one Y. Thus, the combination of these chromosomes, inherited from the parents, determines the sex of the individual. This discovery proved for the first time that sex is set by chromosomes, not by environment or diet as previously thought.

Stevens' key observation: the Y chromosome is about 3 times smaller than the X chromosome. This size difference is visible under the microscope.

Nettie Stevens used special staining techniques to make the chromosomes visible. She examined hundreds of cells to confirm that the Y chromosome was consistently smaller in all males.

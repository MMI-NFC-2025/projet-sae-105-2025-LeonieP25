Nettie Stevens, science pioneer

1861 - 1912

Biography

Born in 1861 in Vermont, Nettie Stevens developed a passion for science from a young age. After teaching, she joined Bryn Mawr College, where she made her mark in a field still closed to women.

Career

At Bryn Mawr, she studied cells and reproduction. Her research on insects led her to explore chromosomal differences between males and females.

Discoveries

In 1905, she proved that sex is determined by the X and Y chromosomes, overturning the beliefs of the time and laying the foundations of modern genetics.

Scientific legacy

She died in 1912, leaving a major scientific legacy. Rediscovered today, Nettie Stevens embodies the belated recognition of women in the history of science.

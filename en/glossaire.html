Glossary

glossary

Key terms

Chromosome
Structure in the cell that contains DNA and thus genes.
DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid)
Molecule that carries the genetic information of all living beings.
Gene
A segment of DNA that determines a trait (like eye color).
Chromosomal sex
How biological sex is defined by X and Y chromosomes (XX = female, XY = male).
Cytology
Science that studies cells and their functioning.
Down syndrome
Genetic disorder caused by an extra chromosome (chromosome 21).
Matilda Effect
Attributing the discoveries of women scientists to men.
Invisibility
Erasure or forgetting of the role of women in history or science.
Forgotten figures
Women scientists whose discoveries were ignored or attributed to men.
Representation
How women are shown in books, media, or textbooks.
Biopic
Film depicting the life of a real person, often a scientist, artist, or historical figure.
Pioneer
A woman who is among the first to accomplish something in a particular field.
Resilience
Ability to overcome obstacles or difficulties, especially in a context of discrimination or marginalization.
Struggle for recognition
Effort made by women to gain visibility and credit in the scientific field.
Gamete
Reproductive cell (sperm or egg) that transmits half of the genetic material to the offspring.
Heredity
Transmission of biological traits from one generation to the next.
Spermatogenesis
Process of sperm formation in males.
Scientific method
Set of rigorous techniques for observing, experimenting, and drawing reliable conclusions.
Sociology of science
Study of practices, structures, and social relations in the scientific field.
Committed work
Book or publication that takes a stand to denounce an injustice or promote social change.
Recognition inequalities
Disparities in the visibility, credit, and appreciation of scientific work according to gender.
Mobile website
Site designed to be viewed mainly on smartphones or tablets, with an interface adapted to small screens.
Navigation
How the user moves and accesses different pages and sections of a website.
Popularization
Simplified explanation of a scientific or technical concept to make it accessible to a wide audience.
Timeline
Visual representation of events or information in chronological order.
Gallery
Section of a website presenting images, videos, or documents in an organized way.
AI (Artificial Intelligence)
Technology capable of generating content or performing tasks usually done by humans.

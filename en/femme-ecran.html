Women of Science on Screen

Short film highlighting Nettie Stevens

“Cherchez la femme!”, Nettie Stevens – The Birth of Genetics – ARTE

The short film “Cherchez la femme – Nettie Stevens”, broadcast on ARTE, highlights the journey of Nettie Stevens, an American geneticist from the early 20th century.

Through concise narration and animated illustrations, the episode retraces her fundamental discovery: the identification of X and Y sex chromosomes as determinants of biological sex. This major breakthrough was long attributed to her male colleagues, but the short film emphasizes the importance of her contribution to science.

In just two minutes, it pays tribute to this often-forgotten scientist, while offering a reflection on the recognition of women in science.

ARTE, 2019
Short film about the life and work of Nettie Stevens.

Series and Additional Media

  • “Radioactive”, Marjane Satrapi, 2019 A vibrant biopic about Marie Curie, tracing her life, major discoveries on radioactivity, and the obstacles she faced as a woman in a male-dominated field.
  • Picture a Scientist, Sharon Shattuck & Ian Cheney, 2020 A powerful documentary giving voice to contemporary female researchers facing sexism and invisibility in science, while celebrating their resilience.
  • “Hidden Figures”, Theodore Melfi, 2016 Inspired by the true story of African-American mathematicians at NASA, whose calculations enabled the first American spaceflights. A strong tribute to perseverance and recognition.
  • Women and Science, ARTE documentary series, 2021 A series exploring the journeys, obstacles, and achievements of women in various scientific fields, from the 19th century to today.

“Find the woman!”, Nettie Stevens – The birth of genetics – ARTE

Short film about the life and work of Nettie Stevens.

ARTE 2019,
The short film “Find the Woman – Nettie Stevens”, broadcast on ARTE, highlights the journey of Nettie Stevens, an American geneticist from the early 20th century.
Through concise narration and animated illustrations, the episode retraces her fundamental discovery: the identification of the X and Y sex chromosomes as determinants of biological sex. This major breakthrough was long attributed to her male colleagues, but the short film emphasizes the importance of her contribution to science. In just two minutes, it pays tribute to this often-forgotten scientist while offering a reflection on the recognition of women in the scientific field.

Series and complementary media

“Radioactive”, Marjane Satrapi, 2019
A vibrant biopic about Marie Curie, tracing her life, her major discoveries in radioactivity, and the obstacles she faced as a woman in a male-dominated environment.
Women and Science, ARTE documentary series, 2021
A series exploring the journeys, obstacles, and achievements of women in various scientific fields, from the 19th century to today.
“Hidden Figures”, Theodore Melfi, 2016
Inspired by the true story of African American mathematicians at NASA whose calculations enabled the first American spaceflights. A powerful tribute to perseverance and recognition.
Picture a Scientist, Sharon Shattuck & Ian Cheney, 2020
A powerful documentary giving voice to contemporary women researchers facing sexism and invisibility in the scientific world, while celebrating their resilience.
